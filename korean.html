Korean To Get You Started!

Korean is the official language of both North and South Korea, and has around 80 million speakers worldwide. Uniquely, historical linguists classify Korean as being totally unrelated to other languages, and so in effect, is its own language family. Korean is not written in the roman alphabet, but uses its own syllabary known as Hangul, which contains 14 consonants and 10 vowels. Luckily for learners, it is said to be among the most phonologically faithful writing systems in use - many Hangul shapes mirroring the shape of the speaker's mouth as they pronounce it!
